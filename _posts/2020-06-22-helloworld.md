Thank you for visiting my website and blog! 

At the time of this post, I am a rising third-year Ph.D. student in Economics at Duke University. I am interested in the fields of labor and public economics, acknowledging that there are many fascinating topics at the intersection. This website will house my updated C.V. and research activities in addition to a blog.

Even though I am a graduate student, I strive to approach my learning and work with a rigorous and principled mindset. This mindset will be reflected, cultivated, and shared in this blog. 

* I aim to have an **organized and intentional workflow**. You can expect posts on workflow tips and tricks that I have experimented with (to varying degrees of success...). 
* I aim to learn freely **without subscribing to methodological ideology**. You can expect posts highlighting papers that combine or contrast different methodlogical and conceptual approaches in an open manner, including papers outside of my immediate interests that I find inspiring.
* I aim to **study policy-relevant questions**. You can expect posts that summarize the implications of policy-relevant economic research with a broader audience in mind.
* Finally, I aim to **be a resource for aspiring economic students**, especially those who identify with groups that are under-represented in economics. You can expect posts that highlight information on careers in economics and graduate school options. Are you an aspiring economist or curious about economics as a career option? Reach out to me directly with your questions. I am happy to share my own experiences or help locate the appropriate resources.

If you have thoughts, reactions, or feedback, I look forward to learning with you either through the comments or by email (anna (dot) ziff (at) duke (dot) edu).
