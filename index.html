Ph.D. Candidate in Economics, Duke University

Research Interests:
  • Primary: Public economics
  • Secondary: Labor economics, applied econometrics, urban economics

My job market paper proposes an approach to study spillover effects of local policies that propagate through latent markets instead of only spatially. While the approach is particularly relevant for place-based economic development, it can be adapted to other contexts.

I exemplify the approach with a particular place-based policy and estimate spillover effects within latent housing markets. To characterize housing markets, I apply an approach in network theory to household moves. The empirical findings inform conclusions on overall effectiveness and redistribution that vary from analysis using only direct treatment effects.

I am on the 2023-2024 job market.
